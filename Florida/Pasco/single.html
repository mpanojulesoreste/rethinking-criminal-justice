Florida

Pasco County

Pasco County spends $120 million into the police budget annually, eclipsing investments in vital areas like education. With a mere 18% of the budget allocated to education, students face an uphill battle to succeed. The county's data, extracted from Pasco County Budget reports, reveals the stark reality of a skewed allocation that perpetuates inequality.

Pasco County's current financial structure not only shortchanges education but also exacerbates social disparities. A striking statistic of 15,000 students enrolled in schools subjected to the notorious "predictive policing" program, as reported by the Tampa Bay Times, underscores the disheartening consequences of a police-focused approach.

Redirecting the police budget into education could yield transformative results. By prioritizing quality education, mentorship, and community programs, Pasco County can break the chains of inequality and uplift its youth. Movements like "Invest in Education, Divest from Policing" advocate for such changes, as evidenced by the data-driven insights provided by The Center for Policing Equity and ACLU Florida.
